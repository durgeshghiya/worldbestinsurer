Health Insurance

Health insurance provides financial support during medical emergencies. When you go through a medical emergency, health insurance pays for the various treatment costs, so you can focus on recovery. It lets you have a financial backup and helps you avoid struggling to secure money in times of emergencies and ill health. [1] It is the financial way of applying the age-old saying ‘prevention is better than the cure’

What is Health Insurance?

In health insurance, the insurance company agrees to pay financial compensation in case a particular event (such as hospitalisation, daycare surgery, medical emergency) happens with the insured. Health insurance can protect you from rising healthcare costs and can help you get quality treatment without draining your savings.

A good health insurance policy pays for key expenses, such as hospitalisation, treatments, medicines, and other medical needs, and gives you access to trusted hospitals across the country. You also get added support like annual health checkups, OPD coverage, diagnostic tests, cashless treatment, preventive care, and tax benefits.

What are the Types of Health Insurance Plans?

Individual Health Insurance

An individual health insurance plan protects one or multiple members with each individual having separate sum insured. It covers hospitalisation, treatments, medicines, and other medical costs based on the value of the sum insured chosen by the buyer. It can suit young professionals and anyone who wants personal financial protection during health emergencies.

View Plans Know more

Family Floater Health Insurance

A family floater plan brings multiple family members under one policy with a shared sum insured. It usually covers a spouse, children, and parents. Since the sum insured is shared, these plans offer good value for families with varied needs. They are easy to manage and ensure that everyone can access care without buying separate policies.

View Plans Know more

Senior Citizen Health Insurance

For people above 60, medical needs rise and costs can rise higher. Senior citizen health insurance plans are designed with this in mind. They cover treatments for age-related illnesses, frequent hospital visits, and longer recovery times. Many plans also include lower waiting periods for certain conditions.

View Plans Know more

Top-Up and Super Top-Up Plans

A top-up or super top-up plan increases your total coverage while keeping premiums affordable. These plans activate after the receival of a chosen deductible amount. They are ideal if you already have basic coverage, either from an employer or an individual plan, and want to boost protection without paying for a full second policy.

Critical Illness Insurance

A critical illness plan pays a lump sum amount when the insured is diagnosed with a listed serious illness such as cancer, heart attack, stroke, kidney failure, or major organ transplant. The payout helps manage treatment costs, income loss, and lifestyle adjustments. It is especially helpful for families with a history of serious illnesses.

Disease-Specific Plans

Some plans focus on particular medical conditions like diabetes, cancer, or cardiac issues. By opting for the best health insurance policy for a diabetic individual or for a cancer patient, you can ensure early-stage and advanced-stage coverage, regular screenings, and support for long-term care.

Women-Centric Plans

Some plans offer wider protection for women-specific illnesses and wellness needs. Women’s health insurance plans are ideal for women planning to start or grow a family.

What is a Mediclaim Policy?

It is a type of health cover that pays for hospitalisation expenses when you are admitted for treatment.

Mediclaim insurance focuses mainly on inpatient care. It covers costs like room rent, doctor visits, medicines, and basic procedures done during the hospital stay. These mediclaim policies are designed to handle immediate medical expenses. A mediclaim policy helps you avoid paying large hospital bills out of pocket.

While coverage is limited when compared to full health insurance, a mediclaim policy still offers essential financial protection during a critical situation.

What Is the Difference between Mediclaim and Health Insurance?

Mediclaim insurance and health insurance are often mixed up, but they serve different purposes.

Mediclaim policy - It is designed to cover hospitalisation expenses only. It pays for costs like room rent, doctor consultations, medicines, and procedures that require you to be admitted. It is a straightforward plan meant for basic medical needs and emergency care.

Health insurance - It offers wider and more flexible protection. A health insurance plan covers hospitalisation but also supports you before and after admission with tests, scans, consultations, and medicines. It includes day care procedures that do not require 24-hour admission and may cover home healthcare, AYUSH treatments, and preventive checkups.

How to Save Tax with Section 80D?

Tax Deduction on the Premium Paid for Self and Family*

When you buy a health insurance plan for yourself, your spouse, and your dependent children, the premium you pay qualifies for a tax deduction under Section 80D. If all the four members are under 60 years of age, the maximum you can claim is ₹25,000 per financial year. [10]

Additional Deduction for Parents

If you pay the medical insurance premium for your parents, you can claim an additional deduction of up to ₹25,000 every financial year. If one or both parents are senior citizens, this limit increases to ₹50,000. [10]

Deduction on Preventive Health Check-ups*

Under Section 80D, you can also claim tax benefits for preventive health check-ups. You can claim up to ₹5,000 each financial year as expenses incurred for these tests. [10]

What are the Health Insurance TermsYou Need to Know About?

Understanding key terms makes it easier to compare health insurance plans and choose the coverage that fits your needs.

1

Dependents

Dependents refer to the family members you can include in health insurance policies, such as your spouse, children, or parents.

2

Deductibles

Deductibles are the fixed amounts you must pay from your pocket before the insurer starts covering your medical expenses. [17]

3

Sum Insured

Sum Insured is the maximum amount your insurer will pay for all claims in a policy year. Choosing the right sum insured is crucial because it determines your financial protection.

4

Co-payment

Co-payment means you share a certain percentage of the medical cost with the insurer. For example, with a 10 percent co-pay, you pay 10 percent of every eligible bill, and the insurer pays the remaining 90 percent. [14]

5

Critical Illness

Critical Illness coverage offers a lump sum payout when you are diagnosed with a listed serious illness such as cancer, heart attack, or kidney failure. The amount helps you manage treatment and lifestyle expenses.

6

Pre-existing Diseases

Pre-existing Diseases are health conditions you already have before buying the policy. PEDs usually come with a waiting period before you can make a claim.

7

Riders

Riders are optional add-ons you can include to enhance your coverage, such as maternity cover, room rent waiver, or OPD benefits.

8

No Claim Bonus (NCB)

No Claim Bonus (NCB) rewards you for not filing any claims during a policy year. The bonus usually increases your sum insured without increasing your premium.

9

Restoration Benefit

Restoration Benefit refills your sum insured if it gets exhausted during the year. It is especially helpful during multiple treatments or back-to-back medical emergencies.

What is the Eligibility Criteria for Buying Health Insurance?

Your eligibility for buying a health insurance policy depends on several factors that help insurers understand your health status and overall risk profile. Knowing them helps you prepare better before applying for a health insurance plan.

Previous Medical Conditions / Pre-Existing Illnesses

Any existing health issues like diabetes, high BP, asthma, thyroid conditions, or past surgeries can affect eligibility. Insurers may apply waiting periods, ask for medical tests, or charge a higher premium depending on the condition.

Age

Younger applicants get quicker approvals, lower premiums, and wider plan choices. Older applicants may face mandatory medical check-ups or limited coverage options.

Lifestyle Habits

Habits such as smoking, high alcohol consumption, or a sedentary lifestyle can increase risk. Insurers may increase premiums or add conditions if your lifestyle raises the chances of future health issues.

Occupation

Jobs that involve physical risk, exposure to hazardous materials, or irregular work hours can also influence eligibility.

BMI and Overall Fitness

Being underweight or overweight can affect eligibility, as they can trigger a higher chance of lifestyle-related conditions.

Insurance Claim History

If you had multiple claims in past policies, some insurers may closely review your application when you are buying a health insurance policy and may limit certain benefits.

These factors help insurers assess your health accurately and tailor a health insurance plan that suits your needs.

Frequently Asked Questions on Health Insurance

  • General
  • Cover
  • Policy
  • Claims
  • Renew

1. What is meant by pre-existing disease?

A pre-existing disease refers to any illness, condition, or injury that you were diagnosed with or treated for in the 48 months before buying a health insurance policy. If you received medical advice, treatment, or had symptoms during this period, it is considered a pre-existing condition under medical insurance rules. [13]

2. How does health insurance portability benefit the policyholders?

Health insurance portability helps you to change your health insurance plan without having to go through a fresh waiting period term. There is a smooth transfer from one insurer to another if your current plan is not sufficient to cover rising medical costs.

3. I have health insurance from my company. Should I also purchase a separate health insurance policy?

Yes, having a separate individual health insurance plan is important. Your employee health insurance covers medical expenses only while you are working in the organization. Once you quit the company, your policy term ends. Keeping medical inflation in mind, it is important to buy personal health insurance as per your medical needs. Also, a corporate health plan is a common plan designed for all employees.

4. What does cashless hospitalisation mean in the context of health insurance?

Cashless hospitalisation is a procedure in which the policyholder does not have to pay medical expenses out of their pocket in case they are admitted to a hospital or have undergone a surgery. However, there are certain deductibles or non-medical expenses at the time of discharge, which are not included in the policy terms, that have to be paid at the time of discharge.

5. What does it mean by pre and post-hospitalisation expenses?

In case you have to undergo a surgery, there are certain pre-hospitalization expenses, such as diagnosis cost, consultations etc. Likewise, post the surgery, there could be expenses to monitor the health of the policyholder. These expenses are known as pre and post hospitalization expenses.

6. How many times can medical insurance be claimed?

You can file multiple claims during a policy term, provided it is within the limit of the sum insured. A policyholder can get coverage up to the sum insured only.

7. Is it possible to buy more than one health insurance policy?

Yes, it is possible to buy more than one medical insurance plan. This entirely depends on an individual’s necessity and coverage requirements.

8. Can we claim medical bills in health insurance?

Yes, you can claim medical bills in health insurance, as long as it is within the sum insured. For more information, read the policy wording document.

9. How long does it take to settle a mediclaim?

It usually takes around 7 working days to settle a (reimbursement) claim if the documents are in order.

10. How to check medical insurance claim status?

You can check your claim status through self-help portals or mobile apps extended by insurers.

11. Before buying health insurance, do I need to undertake any medical examination?

There may be a few medical tests that you need to undergo before buying health insurance. For some health insurance policies, the medical tests are required if there are pre-existing illnesses or if someone is above 40 years of age.

12. What is the process to add my family members to my existing policy?

You can add your family members at the time of purchasing or renewal of your health insurance policy.

13. Can my children be added in my health insurance plan? Also, what should be their age?

Yes, children can be added to your health insurance plan. They can be added post 90 days of birth up to the age of 21 or 25 years. It varies from company to company, so please go through the plan’s eligibility from the product brochure.

14. Are there any benefits of buying health insurance at a young age?

You are eligible to pay a lower premium and higher benefits. Since the probability of having a pre-existing illness is low, the waiting periods may also not impact you. Apart from that, common diseases such as flu or accidental injuries can happen at any age, so buying health insurance when you are young is important.

15. What does a waiting period in health insurance plans mean?

The time span during which you cannot raise a claim to avail some or all benefits of the health insurance from your insurance provider for a specified illness is known as the waiting period. So, basically, you have to wait for a specified amount of time before you request for a claim.

16. What is a ‘Free Look Period’?

During this free look period, you have the option of cancelling your policy without penalty if you feel your policy is not beneficial. Depending on the insurance company and the plan offered, the free look period can be 10-15 days or even longer. Know more on the free look period.

17. What does network/non-network hospitalisation mean?

Network hospitals, better known as cashless hospitals, have an agreement with your insurance company, because of which you can avail cashless hospitalisation benefit. On the other hand, if you get treated in a non-network hospital, you have to pay the bills first and later apply for a reimbursement claim. So, it is always wise to buy a health insurance company which has a large network hospital tie-up.

18. What is meant by domiciliary hospitalisation?

When a policyholder is in a condition that he/she cannot be admitted to a hospital or the treatment is taken at home due to non-availability of rooms in the hospital, it is known as domiciliary hospitalisation.

19. What are the benefits under basic hospitalisation?

In case of a hospitalisation cover, we cover expenses like pre and post hospitalization expenses for your diagnostic tests, consultations and medicine costs. We also cover ICU, bed charges, medicine cost, nursing charges, and operation theatre expenses widely.

20. Is there a right age to buy health insurance policy?

There is no right or wrong age to buy a health insurance policy. However, it is recommended to buy a health plan early to get lower premiums. Once you turn 18 years old, you can buy a health insurance plan for yourself. Before that, a family health insurance plan can cover your healthcare expenses.

21. Can Health Insurance be bought by a minor?

No, a health insurance policy cannot be purchased by a minor. But they can be covered under the family floater health insurance bought by their parents.

22. If I’m admitted to a non-network hospital, what should I do?

In case you get admitted at a non-network hospital, you have to pay the bills from your pocket first and later raise a reimbursement claim with your insurance company. However, your health insurance company will provide reimbursement only up to the amount of the sum insured. 

23. What does the annual sum insured mean?

The annual sum insured is the maximum amount up to which your health insurance will pay for admissible medical expenses during a given policy year. For example, if the annual sum insured is ₹5 lakh and you are hospitalised where the bill amounts to around ₹6 lakh, the insurer will only pay ₹5 lakh.

24. Does a waiting period apply if I increase my sum insured at the time of renewal?

Yes, waiting periods shall apply afresh to the increased portion of the sum insured [SI] amount. Suppose your original sum insured is ₹5 lakh, and the plan had a waiting period of 3 year for declared pre-existing conditions [PED]. After one year, if at renewal you increase the sum insured from ₹5 lakh to ₹15 lakh, then for the original SI ₹5 lakh, the PED waiting period of 2 years shall apply. For the increased ₹10 lakh portion, a fresh PED waiting period of 3 years shall apply.

25. What are the 4 most common health insurance plans?

The four most common medical insurance plans are Individual Health Insurance, which covers one person; Family Floater Plans, covering the entire family under a single sum insured; Critical Illness Plans, which provide a lump sum on diagnosis of chronic illnesses; and Senior Citizen Health Plans, designed specifically for people above 60 years.

1. Are diagnostic charges like X-ray, MRI or ultrasound covered in Health Insurance policies?

Yes. Most health insurance plans cover diagnostic charges during hospitalization, pre-hospitalization and post-discharge as well.

2. Will I get covered for my pre-existing illnesses?

Yes. Once your specified waiting period is over you will get coverage for your pre-existing illnesses. Read this, to know more on coverage for pre-existing diseases.

3. How can I know if my family is covered in my health insurance policy?

You need to check your policy document and enrol your family members by mentioning their name and age to get them covered.

4. When you buy a health insurance policy online, will we get a cashless card?

Buying online health insurance is no different from purchasing it offline. In fact, buying online health insurance is quick and hassle-free. A cashless card via courier/postal services is provided to you. To know more, visit the company website or dial the customer care number.

5. What are the inclusions under hospitalisation expenses?

Important medical expenses such as blood investigations, diagnostic charges such as CT scan, MRI, sonography etc. are covered. In certain cases, hospital room rent, bed charges, nursing charges, medicines and doctor visits, etc. can also be covered.

6. Are robotic surgery & modern treatments covered under health insurance plans?

Yes. It depends on the policy terms and conditions. However, most health insurance companies offer coverage for modern treatments and robotic surgeries.

8. Is home isolation covered under your policy?

No, home isolation is not covered in our health policies. You can only file a claim for medical treatment undertaken at a hospital or nursing home. The treatment should be on the advice of and be actively managed by a qualified doctor.

9. Will COVID-19 tests be covered under the policy?

The testing charges will only be covered in case of hospitalization for each insured member(s) covered under the policy.

1. Is it possible to change the nominee during mid-term of a policy?

It can be done. An endorsement request needs to be raised by the policyholder for a change in nominee details.

2. In case a policy lapses during hospitalization, what happens in that situation?

Do not worry if your policy expires during hospitalization because you get a grace period of 30 days post the policy lapse. However, if you do not renew your policy within the grace period and hospitalization happens after the grace period, then you will have to pay for the medical expenses.

3. I am a foreigner, but my child is pursuing further studies in India. Can I purchase a health insurance policy for my child?

If your child is an Indian citizen, then you can purchase a health insurance policy. If not, you should opt for student travel insurance for your child.

4. Is there any impact on health insurance premiums due to smoking or chewing tobacco?

Tobacco users are prone to higher health risks. If tobacco is consumed in any form, the chances of an individual developing some health issue later in life are higher. [18] This means you are more likely to claim the treatment cost. So, these individuals are categorized as high-risk by the insurance company and high premiums are charged to them.

5. What do you mean by cumulative bonus in a health insurance policy?

The bonus/ reward that one gets for not filing a claim is known as a cumulative bonus. The cumulative bonus benefit is given in the renewal year by increasing the sum insured amount only up to a certain year for every claim-free year. This helps you to get a higher sum insured without paying anything extra. [19]

6. What are health policy discounts?

Many companies may offer a family discount if you cover two or more family members under a single health plan on an individual sum insured basis. Even long-term policy discounts can be availed on buying health insurance for more than 2-3 years. Some insurers also give fitness discounts on renewals.

7. Can my non-Indian friend buy a health insurance policy if he/she not a citizen but living in India?

No. Only Indian citizens can purchase health insurance plans in the country.

8. Can my health insurance be cancelled? In that case, can I get my premium back?

If a health insurance plan is cancelled within the free look period, you will get your premiums refunded, after adjusting the underwriting cost, pre-acceptance medical costs, etc.

1. Is cashless facility available at every network hospital?

Yes. There is a pre-decided agreement between your health insurance company and network hospitals, and hence cashless treatment facility is available at every network hospital..

2. Are there chances of a request for cashless claim authorization to be rejected/ denied?

Yes. A pre-authorization request for cashless claim can be rejected if the policyholder files a claim for an ailment/disease which is either excluded, falls in the waiting period, or if the sum insured has been used up.

3. From the time of an accident, is there a timeline to be followed to file a claim or can it be filed immediately?

In case of reimbursement claims, the insurance company needs to be informed within a period of 30 days post discharge.

4. What is meant by claim settlement ratio?

The percentage of the number of claims that the insurance company paid out during a financial year out of the total claims incurred is known as the claim settlement ratio (CSR). It reflects if the insurer is financially secure enough to pay for its claims.

5. Once a claim is filed, what happens to the policy?

Your policy period continues as usual, but the amount you claimed for gets deducted from your sum insured. After the renewal of the health insurance plan, your sum insured again gets back to the amount you had opted for at the time of renewal.

6. If I take a costlier health insurance policy, will it facilitate privileged treatment in case of hospitalisation and claim?

It depends on the policy terms and conditions. Say, if you have a health cover of ₹1 crore, this helps you take care of all the possible medical expenses.

7. What is the health insurance claim procedure?

A cashless claim request can be raised by contacting the insurance department at the network hospital or your health insurance company. For reimbursement claims, after the discharge, you need to send the invoices to your insurance provider.

8. What is the time period to file a medical insurance claim?

Within 30 days post-discharge. A claim should be raised with the insurance provider as soon as possible, without any delay.

9. What is the waiting period for claims under a policy?

Waiting periods depend on the policy terms and conditions. There is a waiting period for specific ailments/diseases which could be for 2-4 years.

1. How do I renew my mediclaim policy?

You can renew your Mediclaim policy with HDFC ERGO easily by visiting the website or mobile app, selecting your existing health insurance policy, reviewing the coverage, updating details if needed, and making the payment online. Renewal allows you to continue enjoying the benefits under your health insurance plan.

2. Is my health insurance policy transferable without losing renewal benefits?

Yes, you can transfer your health insurance policies without losing renewal benefits.

3. Do I get a discount on the renewal of the policy with the same health insurance policy?

Yes. If you have not made claims, then you get a cumulative bonus, which is an increase in the sum insured without paying for the same. You can also get fitness discounts if your health parameters, such as BMI, diabetes levels, blood pressure levels are improved.

4. If my policy is not renewed in time before expiry date, will it be denied for renewal?

Possibly, yes. If you have not renewed your policy within the grace period, then there are high chances that your policy may lapse.

5. Is it possible to modify my add-on cover while renewing health insurance policy?

Yes. You can add or remove optional/add-on covers at the time of renewal. This is not permitted during the policy tenure. 

6. Is there a Grace Period for Renewal?

Yes. You have a grace period of around 30 days (varies by insurer) within which you can pay the premiums and renew the policy.

7. If I choose to renew my health insurance policy online, how long will it take?

Usually, it takes not more than a few minutes, but you need to keep details, such as your policy number and other information, ready.

9. If I miss the renewal date of my health insurance policy, what should I do?

You get a grace period of 15-30 days to renew your health insurance policy. You need to renew within that period. But if your grace period is also over, your policy will expire. You have to buy a new policy with a fresh waiting period and other benefits.
